Debit card vs credit card: what is the difference

The basic difference is simple: a debit card spends your own money, while a credit card uses the bank's money and must be paid back on time.

When a debit card is better

  • For everyday spending without debt risk.
  • For tighter budgeting with a fixed income.

When a credit card is better

  • For building credit history.
  • For cashback, rewards, and purchase protection.

Common beginner mistakes

  • Using the full credit limit.
  • Paying after the due date.
  • Opening cards only for bonuses without a plan.
